# Phase 1 - Ideate

Goal: end this phase with a one-paragraph project brief the user has confirmed. Do not start picking stacks yet.

## Step 1: Idea or suggestion?

Ask one clear question first: **"Do you already have an idea, or should I suggest a few?"**

## Path A - the user has an idea

Capture it in their words, then stress-test it instead of expanding it. Ask at most three sharp questions:

1. Who is the one person this is for, and what do they do today instead?
2. What is the smallest version that is still useful (the thing you could show someone in a week)?
3. What is the riskiest assumption - the thing that, if false, sinks it?

If the idea is much bigger than a beginner can ship, say so plainly and propose the smallest useful slice. Resist scope. The win is a finished small thing, not an unfinished big thing.

## Path B - suggest ideas

1. Establish the profile cheaply (see SKILL.md "Researching the project and the user"). You need: their skills, time per week, and goal (learn / portfolio / money).
2. Research current relevance with a few **targeted** web searches - not an exhaustive survey. Look for what is in demand or trending near their existing skills. Summarize findings in 2-3 lines, not a report.
3. Propose **2-3 concrete ideas**, each scoped to a realistic first version. For each, one line on why it fits *them* and one line on why it is worth doing *now*. Avoid generic "build a to-do app" suggestions unless tied to a specific angle.
4. Let them pick. Do not pad with more than three.

## Step 2: Write the brief

A single short paragraph: what it is, who it is for, the smallest useful version, and the one success signal. Get an explicit "yes, that's it" before moving to planning.

Token note: a handful of searches, summarized tightly. The brief is a paragraph, not a document.
